Council Member Derek O. Robinson Sr.
Madera City Council District 4<
District 4 Map

Derek Robinson is currently serving his first term in office as a Madera City Council Member. Derek was elected to the City Council in 2012 for a four year term through 2016.

Derek served as Mayor Pro Tem in 2014. The Mayor Pro Tem position is rotated annually amongst the Council Members serving.

Members of the City Council also serve as Board Members of the Successor Agency to the Former Madera Redevelopment Agency and as Commissioners of the Housing Authority of the City of Madera.

Derek graduated from John Muir High School located in Pasadena, California in 1972. After high school, Derek served in the United States Army as a Specialist 4 from 1973 to 1978. After completing his service in the Army, Derek continued his education. He attended Long Beach City College and earned an Associate of Arts (AA) degree in 1979. He then went on to the University of California Berkeley and in 1982 earned a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree in Social Science.

Derek worked for the United States Postal Service in Fresno from 1984 through 2002. He also served as a shop steward in the American Postal Workers Union in Berkeley, California from 1988 to 1990.

Derek’s interest in helping youth is evidenced through his many years of having served not only as a youth counselor but also as a track and field coach.

As a youth counselor, Derek served at various institutional facilities including: the San Francisco Youth Correctional Center from 1984 to 1986; the Byron Boys Ranch, Contra Costa Correctional Facility from 1989 to 1993; and the Fresno Juvenile Correctional Facility from 1995 to 2002.

Derek was very active in track and field from 1978 to 2002 initially as a competing athlete and student coach then on to serving as assistant coach and coach at various high schools and colleges including: Long Beach City College; Saint Mary’s High School in Berkeley, California; Miramonte High School in Orinda, California; Berkeley High School in Berkeley, California; San Joaquin Valley Tomahawks in Fresno, California; Fresno City College in Fresno, California; Kings River Community College in Reedley, California; and Clovis High School in Clovis, California.

Derek has been a resident of Madera since 1992. He is married to Michelle Robinson and they have one daughter. Derek has four other children.

In addition to their regular duties as members of the City Council, Council Members are appointed to serve on other boards as representatives of the City Council. They are also active in community organizations.
